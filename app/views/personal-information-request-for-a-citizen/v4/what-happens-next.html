What happens next

Tell the requestor we'll check we have enough information to identify the citizen in our records and gather the information requested. We'll contact them if we need more information.

Once we have enough information, we can start working on their request. We'll send them a confirmation letter to say that we're dealing with their request. We have 1 calendar month from the date the request was sent to complete it. If we need more time, we'll write to them.

What they'll get

We'll post the information with a letter to the address they asked us to write

The letter will tell them:

  • what information we have sent
  • what, if any, information we cannot send and the reasons why

The letter will also include:

  • a link to the DWP Personal Information Charter (or privacy policy) which tells them about how and why we use their personal information and their rights and responsibilities.
  • a link to a glossary so they can understand abbreviations used in the information
  • what to do if they think something is missing
  • how to complain
